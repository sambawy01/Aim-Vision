# AIMVISION Camera Integration Critique

**Reviewer:** Embedded Firmware Engineer · **Date:** 2026-05-06 · **Source:** `AIMVISION_V1_Sprint_Build_Plan.txt` v1.0

## Open GoPro Reality Check

- **BLE pairing on Hero 13 is fragile in re-pair scenarios.** Open GoPro spec exposes a small set of GATT services (Command, Setting, Query, plus WiFi AP control), but the camera retains a bonded peer list and will silently refuse new pairings until you forcibly clear bonds via the "Unpair" command (BLE service `0xb5f9` write). On iOS, CoreBluetooth caches the bond OS-side as well — your Rust core needs an explicit "forget on both ends" recovery path, not just retry. Hero 13 added 2.4/5 GHz dual-band AP control over BLE, which is new vs Hero 11/12.
- **Wi-Fi AP mode has a hard backgrounding problem on iOS.** When the phone joins the camera AP, iOS marks the network as "no internet" and aggressively tears it down on background. Android (12+) is better but still kills the socket on Doze. Plan must assume the live preview connection drops every time the phone screen sleeps; reconnection logic and BLE-keepalive while Wi-Fi is down is mandatory, not P1.
- **Live preview is a UDP MPEG-TS stream of H.264, ~480p/30fps, with a documented 1-2 second floor of latency.** This is not a coaching-grade live feed. Sprint 7's "24+ fps sustained" gate is achievable, but the 1-3 second latency target in Sprint 20 is the _floor_, not the goal. USB-C on Hero 13 supports UVC ("webcam mode") at 1080p with ~200-400ms latency — that should be the federation-tier path, not "P1 investigate."
- **HTTP API has no formal rate limit but serializes commands.** Sending a settings change while a previous one is in-flight returns 500 or hangs the camera state machine. You need an in-Rust command queue with a single in-flight command and a 2-second watchdog per command. Hilight tags via `/gopro/media/hilight` typically land within 100-400ms but are best-effort — there is no ack that the tag was actually written to the file's GPMF stream until recording stops.

## Multi-Camera Sync Recommendation

Drop hardware genlock — GoPro doesn't expose a genlock pin and Labs firmware doesn't truly genlock, it just timestamps. Use a **hybrid: Open GoPro Labs `!MSYNC` (precision time sync over BLE-broadcast clock) for coarse alignment + audio cross-correlation on the muzzle blast for fine alignment**. Labs firmware on Hero 13 broadcasts a master clock via QR or BLE to slaves; in practice this gets you ~5-15 ms alignment, which is one frame at 60fps and not good enough for triangulated 3D pose on a fast gun swing (which moves ~20°/frame at 60fps = real geometric error). Audio cross-correlation on the shot transient gets you to sub-millisecond per-shot alignment because a 12-gauge muzzle event is a sharp impulse. For calibration use **ChArUco, not plain checkerboard** — it survives partial occlusion which is critical when athletes walk through frame; expect ~2-3 mm reprojection error at 5 m baseline with a 12×9 ChArUco board, which is sufficient for swing-path geometry but not for sub-cm head stability claims. Drift over 1 hour: expect 10-30 ms accumulated clock drift between two Hero 13s on Labs `!MSYNC`; re-anchor with audio every shot (free, you already have shot detection). Bake drift compensation into the Recording entity schema _now_ — `camera_clock_offset_ms` per camera per shot — or you'll refactor it in Sprint 17.

## Outdoor Failure Modes (Ranked by Likelihood)

1. **Thermal shutdown.** Hero 11/12 documented to shut down at 4K60 in ~25 min ambient 25°C; Egypt summer ambient is 35-45°C in shade, 60°C+ direct sun on a black camera body. Hero 13's "Enduro"-style management is improved but still derates. **Mitigation:** drop to 2.7K30 for live capture, white silicone sleeve, mount in shade of the stand canopy, never store camera in direct sun between strings. Add a thermal-state poll to your Camera trait now.
2. **2.4 GHz Wi-Fi congestion at clubs.** Public AP, member phones, range PA systems all live in 2.4. Hero 13 supports 5 GHz AP — **force 5 GHz** in your config; accept the ~10 m range hit by mounting the phone closer or using a USB-C tether for the operator station.
3. **Battery + power.** Hero 13 native USB PD accepts 27W, but continuous 4K with screen drains the Enduro battery in ~70 min. Plan must spec a USB-C PD power bank (≥20Ah, 30W+) on every camera and a powered USB-C hub for the operator phone. D-Tap is overkill for a club, fine for federation rigs.
4. **Audio for shot detection in multi-shooter environments.** Media Mod mic is a cardioid front-facing electret at 48 kHz mono. Two shooters firing within 1.5 sec on adjacent stations will produce overlapping transients that a simple threshold detector will merge. You need a **per-camera audio-localized shot detector with TDOA** between cameras to attribute shots to stations — this is more ML work than the plan accounts for. Wind noise above 15 km/h will dominate; add a foam windscreen as a hardware SKU.
5. **Dust ingress and lens contamination.** Media Mod is _not_ sealed — the 3.5mm jack and HDMI port are dust gates. Plan a daily lens-and-port wipe protocol; spec replacement Media Mods as consumables (~3-month life in dusty range conditions). Polarized clay-paint glare on orange targets: use a CPL filter on the lens (Hero 13 supports threaded mods).

## Mock/Fixture Improvements

1. **Fault-injection fixture grammar.** Mock should consume a YAML script: `t=12.3s drop_wifi for 4s`, `t=18s ble_disconnect`, `t=22s thermal_warn`, `t=45s battery_low`. Replay deterministically in CI. This catches reconnection bugs before Egypt validation, not during it.
2. **Synthetic multi-camera with injected drift.** Two mock cameras, configurable clock skew (constant offset + linear drift + jitter PDF), shared synthetic shot timeline. This is the only way to test your sync code before Sprint 17 hardware arrives — and it's the difference between Sprint 17 being a 2-week or 8-week sprint.
3. **Golden sessions in-repo as Git LFS fixtures.** Three real Egypt sessions (good lighting, harsh sun, dust storm) checked in as compressed MPEG-TS + GPMF + ground-truth shot timestamps. Every CI run replays them through the full pipeline and asserts on shot-detection F1, pose-keypoint stability, and report generation time. Without these you have no regression detection on ML changes.

## Trait Design Changes for Vendor Flexibility

1. **Split the trait into `CameraControl` + `CameraTransport` + `CameraMedia`.** Right now "Camera trait" implies one fat object. Insta360 X4 has fundamentally different control semantics (it's a 360 stitch, not a flat sensor) — you need `CameraCapabilities` returned at connect time so the app can ask "do you support live preview? Hilight? external trigger?" rather than calling and getting `Err(Unsupported)`. This is the single biggest refactor cost you'll eat in V3 if you skip it.
2. **First-class `TimeSource` trait alongside `Camera`.** Don't bolt timecode onto camera methods. A phone-as-camera or future custom AIMVISION hardware will have wildly different clock disciplines (PTP, NTP, GPS-disciplined). Bake `fn time_source(&self) -> &dyn TimeSource` into Sprint 3.
3. **Capability-typed events, not stringly-typed events.** Hero 13 emits "thermal_warning", "battery_low", "sd_full"; Insta360 emits different ones; custom hardware will emit "imu_calibration_drift". Use a sealed enum with `CameraEvent::Vendor(VendorEvent)` escape hatch so the app layer compiles against a stable surface.

## Critical Things Missing

1. **Firmware version pinning + tested matrix.** GoPro pushes Hero 13 firmware roughly every 2 months and _does_ break the HTTP API surface (e.g., setting IDs renumbered between Hero 11 v01.20 and v02.00). Pin the camera at a known-good firmware in Sprint 4, document the matrix `{camera_model, firmware_version → app_version}`, and add a startup check that refuses to start a session on untested firmware with a clear "update app or downgrade firmware" message. The Sprint 12 "firmware update detection" line is not enough.
2. **GoPro Labs firmware path.** Labs unlocks `!MSYNC`, scheduled capture, QR-code control, GPS lock — features stock Hero 13 doesn't have. Federation tier almost certainly needs Labs. Decide in Sprint 3, not Sprint 17, whether you support Labs firmware (and accept GoPro can revoke it) or stay stock-only (and accept worse multi-cam sync).
3. **External trigger / GPIO.** Hero 13 USB-C does _not_ expose GPIO; the only "trigger" path is BLE shutter command (~80-150 ms latency, jittery). For a competition timer integration (V1.5+) you'll want hardware trigger — investigate the GoPro Wired Remote pinout (it's a custom 2.5mm jack on older mods, USB-C HID on Hero 13) now, before the V2 hardware roadmap solidifies.
4. **Audio config explicit in trait.** Plan never specifies sample rate (48 kHz), channel count (mono on Media Mod, stereo on some mods), bit depth (16-bit), or whether the shot detector runs on raw PCM or on AAC-decoded frames. AAC introduces 20-40 ms encoder latency that matters for sub-shot timing. Capture raw PCM via UVC audio over USB-C if possible.
5. **Mounting hardware and vibration.** A shotgun station has a concrete pad; no vibration there. But if any camera is mounted on the _shooter's_ gun (V2 hints at this), recoil is 30-50G impulse — GoPro housings survive but pose estimation will jitter. Plan needs a hardware spec section: tripod model (Manfrotto 055 class minimum for stability in wind), mount torque spec, anti-vibration isolators for any gun-mounted future SKU, and a documented re-calibration trigger when a camera is bumped (IMU delta threshold from Hero 13's onboard IMU, accessible via GPMF).
